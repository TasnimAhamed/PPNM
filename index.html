Why compare fuel prices?

Comparing prices provides you with a method to find the cheapest fuel near you, which ultimately helps you save money. While it may seem like a couple of pennies per litre, the savings can quickly add up especially when you have a large vehicle. Which is more cash in your pocket which is always nice!

Why have prices gone up?

There are a number of reasons for this one of the major factors is that it current costs suppliers more to buy crude oil to make the required fuels. Another reason is due to the UK's move to the greener E10 petrol in September 2021. E10 fuel has more ethanol in it, which is more expensive than petrol.
